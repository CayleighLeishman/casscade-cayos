What Is CSS Architecture?

Let's say you were the type of person to say "I’m gonna build a castle! It’ll have a tower, a slide, and a secret treasure room!"

That’s architecture — it’s the big plan in your head. It tells you what parts you need, how they work together, and where they go. You’re organizing your dream!

In websites, this means planning:

  • What pages or parts you’ll have (home, about, contact)
  • How the pages connect
  • What each part does (header, card, footer, etc.)

Think of CSS architecture like building with LEGOs. If you throw all the pieces in one big pile, it gets messy real fast. But if you sort your bricks by color and shape, you can build castles without everything falling apart. CSS architecture is just a fancy way of saying, “Let’s keep our styles neat and tidy!”

What Are Components?

Components are like little sections on a website that you can reuse again and again. Imagine a header that shows a preview of an article — that’s a component! You can put it anywhere you want on your site.

Each component has its own picture, title, and button, but they all follow the same rules so they look and work the same.

The best part? When you change a component once, it changes everywhere it’s used on the website — so you don’t have to fix it in lots of places! It’s like magic that keeps your site neat and easy to update. 

<section class="summary">
  <img class="summary__img" />
  <div class="summary__content">
    <h2 class="summary__title">Hello World!</h2>
    <p class="summary__lead">This is a summary card.</p>
  </div>
</section>

Meet BEM: The Naming Superhero

BEM is a super helpful way to name your classes. It stands for:

  • Block – the big thing (like a card)
  • Element – little parts of the big thing (like the title inside the card)
  • Modifier – a twist! (like a bigger or fancier card)

So instead of calling everything “box” or “header2,” you give names that make sense! It’s like labeling your bins so you know where the envelopes go compared to the pens.

Why Should You Care?

When you use BEM and components, your code becomes:

  • Easy to read
  • Super reusable
  • Protected from random styling accidents

  font-size: 1.2rem;
  color: #333;
}
